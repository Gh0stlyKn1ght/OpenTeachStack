## The Teacher Problem

A safety checklist that never says stop is not a safety checklist.

It is decoration.

## The Idea

This checkpoint asks whether your checklist can block unsafe work.

## Classroom Scenario

An AI agent starts editing files outside the agreed folder. The checklist says to stop and review before continuing.

That is the checklist doing its job.

## Weak Version

```text
I wrote safety reminders.
```

Reminders are useful, but stop rules are stronger.

## Better Version

```text
I named the exact conditions where I pause, stop, or ask for review.
```

## Build Step

Add stop conditions:

- unrelated files changed
- private data appears
- command effect is unclear
- source of truth is uncertain
- generated content replaced authored work
- verification fails
- published route behaves differently than expected

## Quality Check

Your checklist should include at least three stop conditions.

## Safety / Accessibility / Source Note

If a safety check fails, do not publish and do not keep prompting around the failure. Fix the boundary first.

## Reflection

Which stop condition will be hardest for you to honor under deadline pressure?

## Capstone Connection

Save the revised checklist. It protects the final Starter Kit and future pathway work.

## Checkpoint Decision Language

Use this checkpoint to make a plain decision about **Chapter Checkpoint**:

- **Ready** means the artifact can be used by another teacher without a side explanation from you.
- **Revise** means the idea is right, but the artifact still needs clearer steps, labels, evidence, or safer wording.
- **Blocked** means you cannot honestly finish the artifact yet because a tool, source, permission, example, or decision is missing.

## Evidence to Save

Save evidence that another teacher could inspect: a screenshot, copied command output, file path, short reflection note, or before-and-after excerpt. If the evidence is only in your memory, the checkpoint is not ready yet.

## Next Revision Trigger

Name the owner and trigger before you move on. Write who will revise it, when they will revisit it, and what would cause the next revision: a broken link, confusing student direction, missing source, unsafe permission, or a teammate review note.
