# Reviewer Notes

## AI Verification Reviewer Notes

Use these notes when reviewing an **AI verification checklist** row from OTS-101.

## Ready

- The original AI claim is visible.
- The teacher names the source, standard, or classroom test used to check the claim.
- The decision is labeled accepted, revised, or rejected.
- The revised wording is student-safe and teacher-owned.
- The row states that no real student names, emails, grades, or private classroom details were used.
- The revision trigger explains when the claim should be checked again.

## Revise

- The claim is plausible but not tied to a source or classroom test.
- The teacher decision is present, but the revised student-facing wording is missing.
- The privacy boundary says "no student data" but does not explain what sample data was used instead.
- The review trigger is vague, such as "check later."

## Rejected

- The AI output invents a source, standard, quote, screenshot, or fact.
- The prompt or output includes real student identifiers.
- The output recommends publishing private classroom work.
- The teacher cannot explain what evidence would prove or disprove the claim.

## Sample Reviewer Comment

> Revise: the teacher correctly rejected the "accurate at any angle" claim, but the checklist needs the final student-facing wording and the fake-data test result before classroom use.
