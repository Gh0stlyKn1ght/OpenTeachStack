## Student-facing lesson

Title: Safe Robot Startup and Shutdown

Learning target: I can use the startup and shutdown checklist before operating the robot.

Why this matters: Robots can move unexpectedly when setup steps are skipped. Safe teams use the same routine every time.

Steps:

1. Read the four-step startup routine.
2. Label the next safe action in each scenario.
3. Practice the routine with a powered-off robot.
4. Submit one scenario response and one sentence explaining the safest decision.

Quick check: Which step prevents accidental motor movement?

## Assignment

Assignment title: Startup Routine Scenario Check

Student task: Read two fictional robot setup scenarios. For each scenario, choose the next safe action and explain why.

Submission: Completed scenario sheet.

## Rubric

| Criteria | Strong | Needs revision |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Correct step | Names the correct next step from the routine. | Names a step that does not match the routine. |
| Explanation | Explains the safety reason in plain language. | Gives a vague or unrelated reason. |
| Vocabulary | Uses startup, shutdown, enable, or power correctly. | Misuses safety vocabulary. |

## Reusable AI course-content prompt

```text
Source:
Use only the approved four-step robot startup and shutdown routine provided by the teacher.

Context:
High school Intro to Robotics Safety mini-unit. First-year robotics students are preparing to practice with a powered-off robot before live operation.

Task:
Draft three short fictional scenarios where students choose the next safe startup or shutdown action.

Format:
Return a table with scenario, student question, expected answer, and checklist step.

Example to imitate:
The robot is on blocks. The controller connects. A teammate reaches into the frame to adjust a wire. What is the next safe action?

Non-example to avoid:
"Robots are powerful, so always be careful."

Checks:
Do not invent safety rules, equipment policy, emergency procedures, or grading criteria. Flag any safety wording the teacher should verify before students use it.
```
