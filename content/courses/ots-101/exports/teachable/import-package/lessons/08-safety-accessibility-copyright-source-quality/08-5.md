> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

Before publishing, make the decision explicit.

Do not rely on "looks fine."

Use the checklist and choose a status.

<FrameworkBlock title="Publish-readiness decision" eyebrow="Checkpoint">
  <ConceptCard title="Ready">
    Safety, accessibility, source, and reuse checks are complete enough for teacher-approved use.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Revise">
    The content is useful, but one or more issues must be fixed before students see it.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Do not publish">
    The content contains unresolved risk, inaccessible design, weak sources, or reuse problems.
  </ConceptCard>
</FrameworkBlock>

## Choose ready when...

Choose ready when:

- private student data is not exposed
- links and tools are checked
- directions are readable and organized
- students can find materials and submission steps
- media has enough explanation or access support
- important facts and summaries are verified
- reuse choices are appropriate for the context
- the teacher can explain why the content is safe enough to use

Ready does not mean perfect.

It means responsibly reviewed.

## Choose revise when...

Choose revise when:

- a link needs replacing
- directions need headings or bullets
- a media item needs explanation
- an example needs fictionalizing
- a source needs attribution
- an AI summary needs correction
- a task asks for unnecessary personal disclosure

Revision is normal. The checklist is doing its job.

## Choose do not publish when...

Choose do not publish when:

- student data is exposed
- a task depends on unsafe or inappropriate links
- source claims cannot be verified
- students cannot access required materials
- copyrighted or restricted material is being reposted carelessly
- the lesson depends on sensitive disclosure without a clear educational need
- the teacher cannot tell what still needs verification

Do not publish is a protection decision, not a failure.

## Final review note

Write a short note:

```text
Safety and access decision: Ready / Revise / Do not publish
Reason:
Required fix before publishing:
Teacher verification still needed:
```

Example:

```text
Safety and access decision: Revise
Reason: The lesson is clear, but the source video requires account access and the image has no explanation.
Required fix before publishing: Replace the video with an accessible source and add image context.
Teacher verification still needed: Check image license and source attribution.
```

## What to keep for the capstone

Save the checklist with your course materials.

Your final Mini Course Content Packet should include evidence that student-facing materials were checked for safety, accessibility, source quality, and responsible reuse.

<TakeawayStrip tone="teacher">
  The goal is not to scare teachers away from publishing. The goal is to publish with enough care that students can trust the course page in front of them.
</TakeawayStrip>
