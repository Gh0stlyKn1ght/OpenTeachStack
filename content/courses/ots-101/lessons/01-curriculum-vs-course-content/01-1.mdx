## The problem

AI can write curriculum language that sounds official before the teacher has made the real instructional decisions.

That is how you get phrases like:

> Students will understand robotics safety and demonstrate responsible behavior.

It sounds fine. It does not yet tell the teacher what students must do, what evidence counts, what safety boundary matters, or what comes next.

## The concept

Curriculum is the plan behind the student-facing materials.

It answers questions like:

- What should students learn?
- What sequence gets them there?
- What evidence proves the learning?
- What must be assessed?
- What needs support, adaptation, or reteaching?
- What should change after this is taught?

AI can suggest options for those decisions.

It should not quietly make those decisions for the teacher.

## Concrete example

For a robotics safety mini-unit, a curriculum decision might be:

> Students can explain and perform a four-step startup and shutdown routine using the class safety checklist before touching powered robot equipment.

That statement does more than name a topic.

It tells the teacher the learning target, student action, evidence, tool, and safety boundary.

## Common misunderstanding

A common weak version is:

> Students will learn robot safety.

That is a topic, not a curriculum decision.

It does not tell the teacher what to teach, what students practice, or what evidence would prove readiness.

## Better mental model

Think of curriculum as the decision layer.

## Quick check

Before asking AI to draft anything student-facing, write four lines:

```txt
Learning target:
Evidence students produce:
Boundary the teacher must verify:
What AI may help draft:
```

If those lines are blank, do not ask AI for the final lesson yet.

Ask it for options, questions, or possible structures instead.

## Boundary note

Do not ask AI to invent standards alignment, school safety policy, grading rules, legal requirements, or district expectations.

If those matter, provide the approved source yourself or mark the item as needing verification.
