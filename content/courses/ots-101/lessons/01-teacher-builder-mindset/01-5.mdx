# Build Task: Teacher Workflow Audit

The chapter build task is a teacher workflow audit. The goal is to choose one repeated teaching workflow and make it visible enough to improve.

Do not audit your whole professional life. Pick one workflow that repeats often and creates friction: building a lesson, posting materials, checking sources, preparing a quiz, collecting student work, revising slides, or finding last year's files.

## Build The Audit

Create a short document, table, or note with these fields:

- Workflow name
- When it happens
- Inputs needed
- Tools or folders used
- Repeated steps
- Places where work gets lost or duplicated
- Privacy, accessibility, or source risks
- One small improvement to test next time

The improvement should be realistic. Examples include creating a source note template, moving final lesson assets into a course folder, renaming draft files consistently, adding an accessibility check before publishing, or creating a single "after teaching" revision note.

## Evidence of Completion

Your audit is complete when another teacher could read it and understand the workflow without sitting next to you. It should show what starts the workflow, what artifact comes out of it, where the files live, and what one improvement you will try.

## Reflection

Answer these questions in two or three sentences:

- Which part of the workflow creates the most avoidable friction?
- Which file, folder, prompt, or template would make the next version easier?
- What should be archived so this work does not have to be rediscovered later?

## Verification Check

Remove or replace student names, grades, private emails, hidden assessment keys, and account details before saving the audit in a reusable course folder.
