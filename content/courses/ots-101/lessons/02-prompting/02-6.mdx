# Prompting for Student-Facing Directions

## Why This Matters

A teacher building a first reusable mini-unit needs a system another educator can inspect. Student-facing directions are where vague planning becomes classroom confusion. AI can help make directions clearer, but only if the teacher supplies the task, materials, limits, and evidence.

## Direction Prompt

Use this structure:

> Rewrite these directions for [grade/course] students. Keep the task the same. Use short sentences, numbered steps, and clear verbs. Include what students submit. Do not add new requirements. Mark any unclear teacher decision as a question instead of guessing.

Example teacher note:

> Students use the sensor to test distances and decide when robot stops. Need table and reflection.

Better prompt:

> Rewrite this for grade 8 robotics students. Students will test five distances with an ultrasonic sensor, record readings in centimeters, choose a stop threshold, and write two sentences explaining the choice. Use numbered steps and include what to submit. Do not add extra materials or change the task.

## What To Check

Review the output for:

- verbs students can act on,
- materials named before students need them,
- submission directions,
- timing or grouping,
- vocabulary students have learned,
- accessibility for readers who need direct language.
