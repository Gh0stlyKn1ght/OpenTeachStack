# Prompt Revision and Teacher Voice

## Why This Matters

A teacher building a first reusable mini-unit needs a system another educator can inspect. AI drafts often sound smooth but generic. Teacher voice is not decoration; it carries classroom expectations, care, pacing, and local judgment.

Revision is the point where AI-assisted text becomes teacher-owned text.

## What Teacher Voice Does

Teacher voice helps students know:

- what matters most,
- how to start,
- what quality looks like,
- when to ask for help,
- how the task connects to previous learning.

Generic AI text often says:

> In this exciting activity, students will explore the fascinating world of sensors.

Teacher-owned text might say:

> Today your job is to find out whether one sensor reading is enough to trust a robot's stopping point. It probably is not, and that is the point of the lab.

## Revision Prompt

Use AI to help revise, not erase your voice:

> Review this student-facing direction draft. Identify lines that are vague, too formal, too cheerful, or not specific enough for my classroom. Suggest revisions, but preserve the facts, task, materials, and teacher tone. Mark any place where you need more context instead of inventing details.
