# Chapter Checkpoint

## Core Idea

Prompting for Teachers is about prompt structure, teacher voice, task constraints, and revision routines for useful AI-assisted drafting. In this section, **Chapter Checkpoint** turns that larger chapter focus into one concrete teacher move.

This section stands on its own inside the OTS-101 course book and does not depend on a reused standalone lesson body.

The work here should help the teacher decide what the prompt must specify before an AI draft is worth reviewing. It should also leave behind evidence that can be reused in the chapter artifact: **Reusable prompt library**.

## Verification Check

- Do not include student-identifiable information, private account details, hidden assessment keys, or private school files.
- Check for the chapter risk: accepting fluent output before the teacher has defined audience, source, format, and quality criteria.
- the revision decision is specific enough to act on
- Use official sources or local policy when software behavior, licensing, safety, or standards interpretation matters.
- Keep the artifact small enough that another teacher could review it without hidden context.
