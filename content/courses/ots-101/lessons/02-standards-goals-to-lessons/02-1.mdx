## The problem

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

The tool is loud.

AI makes it easy to say:

- make me slides
- make me a worksheet
- make me a quiz
- make me a lesson
- make me a rubric

Those requests can produce useful pieces, but they can also produce content that does not prove anything important.

## The concept

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

A learning target names what students should be able to do by the end of the lesson.

It should be clear enough that:

- students understand the goal
- the teacher can plan practice
- the assignment can collect evidence
- the rubric can score the right thing
- AI can draft a narrow support piece

The target decides the tool.

Not the other way around.

## Concrete example

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

Tool-first request:

> Create a quiz about robot startup safety.

Target-first request:

> My learning target is: students can choose the next safe action in a robot startup scenario. Suggest one practice activity and one quick check that would prove this target without inventing safety policy.

The second request gives AI a lane.

It also gives the teacher a way to reject output that does not match the target.

## Common misunderstanding

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

A quiz is not automatically evidence.

A slide deck is not automatically instruction.

A worksheet is not automatically practice.

The artifact only matters if it helps students produce evidence of the target.

## Better mental model

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

Use this order:

```txt
target -> evidence -> practice -> artifact -> AI draft -> teacher verification
```

If you reverse the order, the tool starts driving the lesson.

## Boundary note

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

When the target involves safe physical action, account access, student privacy, grading, or policy, AI may draft practice language but the teacher must verify the actual rules.
