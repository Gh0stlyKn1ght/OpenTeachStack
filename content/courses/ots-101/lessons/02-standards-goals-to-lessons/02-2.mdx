## The confusion

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

Standards are often written for adults.

They may be useful for curriculum planning, but they are not always useful as student-facing lesson language.

Students need to know what they are doing today.

## Side-by-side comparison

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

A standard or goal usually needs translation before it becomes a lesson target.

| Adult-facing wording | Student-facing outcome |
| --- | --- |
| Demonstrate safe operation procedures | I can follow the startup checklist before powering the robot. |
| Evaluate information for credibility | I can explain whether a source supports an AI-generated claim. |
| Apply design constraints | I can choose a design option and explain which constraint shaped my choice. |
| Communicate technical reasoning | I can describe my decision using evidence from the task. |

The student version should preserve the intent, not water it down.

## Weak version

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

> I can understand robot safety.

That sounds student-friendly, but it is too soft.

The teacher cannot see understanding directly.

## Better version

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

> I can choose the next safe startup step and explain which checklist item supports my choice.

This gives the student an action and gives the teacher evidence.

## Decision rule

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

A student-friendly outcome should include three pieces:

1. an action students can perform
2. the concept or tool they use
3. evidence the teacher can review

If one piece is missing, the target probably needs another pass.

## Takeaway

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

AI can help translate wording.

The teacher has to check the meaning.

Do not claim standards alignment just because AI said the language sounds aligned.

## Problem

Turn Standards Into Student-Friendly Outcomes matters because AI can make weak course work look complete. The teacher still has to decide what students should learn, what evidence counts, and what belongs in front of the class.

## Common Misunderstanding

The common mistake is thinking a polished AI draft means the curriculum decision is finished. A draft can be useful and still be unready for students.

## Concrete Example

Instead of asking AI to "make a lesson," the teacher names the learning target, the student task, and the evidence to check. AI can then help draft language inside those boundaries.
