## When to use this workflow

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

Use this workflow when your starting point is not a formal standard.

Sometimes the starting point is messier:

- students keep skipping setup steps
- the last lab was chaotic
- students copy AI output without checking it
- the rubric does not match the assignment
- students can do the task live but cannot explain it

Those are real teaching signals.

They still need to become a sequence.

## Key Idea

A messy classroom problem can become a teachable sequence when the teacher names the target, the practice, and the evidence before asking AI to draft anything.

## Example run

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

Messy idea:

> Students keep rushing robot startup.

Teachable sequence:

1. Students identify why startup order matters.
2. Students label the next safe action in a fictional scenario.
3. Students practice the routine with powered-off equipment.
4. Students explain which checklist step prevents accidental movement.

## What can go wrong

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

Weak sequence:

> Talk about safety, show a video, give a quiz.

This may fill time, but it does not create a clear path from confusion to evidence.

The student may remember safety words without being able to choose a safe action.

## Review gate

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

A teachable sequence should answer:

- What problem started this lesson?
- What target did the problem become?
- What practice comes before assessment?
- What evidence proves the target?
- What can AI draft safely?
- What must the teacher verify?

Ask AI to draft only the practice activity or scenario, not the whole lesson.

## Problem

Turn a Messy Idea Into a Teachable Sequence matters because AI can make weak course work look complete. The teacher still has to decide what students should learn, what evidence counts, and what belongs in front of the class.

## Common Misunderstanding

The common mistake is thinking a polished AI draft means the curriculum decision is finished. A draft can be useful and still be unready for students.

## Concrete Example

Instead of asking AI to "make a lesson," the teacher names the learning target, the student task, and the evidence to check. AI can then help draft language inside those boundaries.
