## The teacher problem

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

AI drafts are easier to review when the teacher has a map.

Without a map, every output looks possible.

The learning target and lesson map gives AI a job and gives the teacher a way to judge whether the output belongs in the course.

## The artifact

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

This map tells AI what to draft and tells the teacher what to check.

## Build step

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

1. Choose one source goal, standard, or messy classroom need.
2. Write the student-friendly target.
3. Name the lesson activity.
4. Name the evidence students produce.
5. Name one narrow AI support task.
6. Name one teacher verification task.
7. Add the next revision trigger.

## Quality check

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

- [ ] The target is student-friendly.
- [ ] The activity produces evidence.
- [ ] The AI support task is narrow enough to review.
- [ ] The teacher verification task is explicit.
- [ ] The next revision is tied to a real teaching moment.

## Boundary note

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

Do not paste private standards documents, student data, or local safety policy into AI unless you have permission.

Use approved public text or summarize only what can safely be shared.
