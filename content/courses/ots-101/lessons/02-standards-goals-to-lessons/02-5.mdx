## What you are checking

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

You are checking whether the target and lesson map can become student-facing content.

Teachers can understand their own intent even when the page is unclear.

Students cannot read the teacher's mind.

## Review criteria

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

A student should be able to answer:

| Student question | Evidence in the map |
| --- | --- |
| What am I learning? | Student-friendly target |
| What am I doing? | Lesson activity |
| What example should I follow? | Example or model plan |
| What do I submit or show? | Evidence |
| What does success look like? | Check or rubric direction |
| What if I am stuck? | Support path or teacher note |

## Self-audit

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

Read your map from a student point of view.

Write answers to these five questions:

1. What do students do first?
2. What example do they use?
3. What do they produce?
4. What does success look like?
5. What happens if they are stuck?

If any answer is missing, revise the map before asking AI for a full lesson draft.

## Revise / keep / remove decision

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

| If the map has... | Decision |
| --- | --- |
| clear target and evidence | keep |
| vague student action | revise |
| activity that does not match target | revise or remove |
| AI task that is too broad | narrow it |
| missing verification note | add it |
