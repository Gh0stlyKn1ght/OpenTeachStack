# Chapter Checkpoint

## Core Idea

AI Literacy and Verification is about verification habits that separate AI fluency from teacher judgment, source trust, and classroom readiness. In this section, **Chapter Checkpoint** turns that larger chapter focus into one concrete teacher move.

This section stands on its own inside the OTS-101 course book and does not depend on a reused standalone lesson body.

The work here should help the teacher decide what needs a source check, privacy check, bias check, or teacher rewrite. It should also leave behind evidence that can be reused in the chapter artifact: **AI verification checklist**.

## Evidence of Completion

## Verification Check

- Do not include student-identifiable information, private account details, hidden assessment keys, or private school files.
- Check for the chapter risk: letting confident generated language replace accountable instructional decisions.
- the revision decision is specific enough to act on
- Use official sources or local policy when software behavior, licensing, safety, or standards interpretation matters.
- Keep the artifact small enough that another teacher could review it without hidden context.
