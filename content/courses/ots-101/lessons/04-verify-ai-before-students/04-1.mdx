# AI Drafts Are Not Teacher Approval

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

An AI draft is not a finished lesson.

It is a proposal.

That distinction matters because AI can produce confident language without understanding your students, your school context, your course sequence, or your professional obligations.

The teacher remains the approver.

## Why This Matters

Students experience the approved course page, not the AI chat that produced it. If the teacher skips review, the draft's hidden mistakes become classroom materials.

## The dangerous shortcut

A risky workflow looks like this:

1. Ask AI for a lesson.
2. Copy the output.
3. Paste it into the course.
4. Let students use it.

That workflow skips the most important step: teacher review.

If the draft is wrong, vague, inaccessible, or unsafe, the students experience the mistake directly.

## A safer workflow

A better workflow looks like this:

1. Ask AI for one bounded draft.
2. Compare the draft against the source and learning target.
3. Revise for student clarity and classroom fit.
4. Check privacy, accessibility, and safety.
5. Publish only the teacher-approved version.

## What approval means

Teacher approval does not mean the content is perfect.

It means the teacher has checked the parts that matter before students interact with it.

Approval means:

- the learning target is still clear
- the student task is understandable
- the source information is accurate enough for the lesson
- the activity fits the time and tools available
- private student information is not exposed
- accessibility barriers have been considered
- the teacher knows what students will produce

## Keep the draft visible

When possible, keep a short note about what changed between the AI draft and the approved version.

This can be simple:

```text
AI draft changed:
- removed invented link
- simplified student directions
- added source citation
- changed discussion task into written evidence check
- added accessibility option for audio response
```

That note helps the teacher learn from the review process.

It also keeps the course from becoming a pile of pasted AI output with no memory of why decisions were made.

<TakeawayStrip tone="warning">
  The moment students see the content, it is no longer just an AI draft. It is your instructional material.
</TakeawayStrip>

## Problem

AI Drafts Are Not Teacher Approval matters because AI can make weak course work look complete. The teacher still has to decide what students should learn, what evidence counts, and what belongs in front of the class.

## Common Misunderstanding

The common mistake is thinking a polished AI draft means the curriculum decision is finished. A draft can be useful and still be unready for students.

## Concrete Example

Instead of asking AI to "make a lesson," the teacher names the learning target, the student task, and the evidence to check. AI can then help draft language inside those boundaries.
