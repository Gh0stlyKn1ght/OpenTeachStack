# Build Artifact: AI Draft Verification Checklist

In this lesson, you will build the checklist you use before students see AI-assisted course content.

This checklist is not meant to slow every lesson to a crawl.

It is meant to make review visible and repeatable.

## What the checklist should catch

Your checklist should help you catch:

- inaccurate claims
- broken or inappropriate links
- invented citations
- unclear student directions
- missing learning evidence
- privacy issues
- accessibility barriers
- tool instructions that do not match reality
- content that sounds finished but does not fit the course

<FrameworkBlock title="Checklist sections" eyebrow="Artifact blueprint">
  <ConceptCard title="Target fit">
    Does the draft support the learning target and expected evidence?
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Student clarity">
    Can students understand what to do, use, create, and submit?
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Source accuracy">

  </ConceptCard>

## Step 1: Add the lesson identity

Start with a small header.

```text
Lesson or artifact name:
Course or class:
Learning target:
AI tool used:
Date reviewed:
Teacher reviewer:
```

This keeps the checklist attached to a real artifact, not a vague memory.

## Step 2: Check target fit

Add these questions:

```text
Target fit:
- Does the draft match the learning target?
- Does the student task require the intended thinking or skill?
- Does the activity produce evidence I can review?
- Does the draft fit the lesson sequence?
```

If the draft does not match the target, revise before checking smaller details.

## Step 3: Check student clarity

Add these questions:

```text
Student clarity:
- Can students tell where to start?
- Are directions written in student-facing language?
- Are materials, links, and tools named clearly?
- Is the expected product or submission clear?
- Are quality expectations visible?
```

If students cannot act on the directions, the draft is not ready.

## Step 4: Check sources and claims

Add these questions:

```text
Source accuracy:
- Are important facts checked against a trusted source?
- Do links open and lead to the intended place?
- Do citations actually support the claims?
- Are software steps current and possible in our environment?
- Did AI invent anything that should be removed or verified?
```

This is where polished AI language gets tested against reality.

## Step 5: Check privacy, accessibility, and safety

Add these questions:

```text
Safety and access:
- Does the draft avoid real private student data?
- Are examples fictionalized or safe to share?
- Can students access the materials and tools?
- Are directions readable and navigable?
- Are alternatives or supports needed?
- Are copyright or usage concerns present?
```

This section protects students and protects the teacher's workflow.

## Step 6: Decide the status

End with a decision.

```text
Review decision:
[ ] Publish after teacher edits
[ ] Revise and review again
[ ] Discard and restart

Revision notes:

Teacher verification needed:
```

A checklist without a decision can become paperwork.

A checklist with a decision helps the teacher move.

## Finished checklist template

```text
AI Draft Verification Checklist

Lesson or artifact name:
Course or class:
Learning target:
AI tool used:
Date reviewed:
Teacher reviewer:

Target fit:
[ ] Draft matches the learning target.
[ ] Student task requires the intended thinking or skill.
[ ] Activity produces evidence the teacher can review.
[ ] Draft fits the lesson sequence.

Student clarity:
[ ] Students can tell where to start.
[ ] Directions are written in student-facing language.
[ ] Materials, links, and tools are named clearly.
[ ] Expected product or submission is clear.
[ ] Quality expectations are visible.

Source accuracy:
[ ] Important facts are checked against a trusted source.
[ ] Links open and lead to the intended place.
[ ] Citations support the claims they are attached to.
[ ] Software steps are current and possible in this environment.
[ ] AI-invented or unsupported details are removed or flagged.

Safety and access:
[ ] No private student data is exposed.
[ ] Examples are fictionalized or safe to share.
[ ] Students can access the materials and tools.
[ ] Directions are readable and navigable.
[ ] Accessibility supports or alternatives are included where needed.
[ ] Copyright or usage concerns are addressed.

Review decision:
[ ] Publish after teacher edits
[ ] Revise and review again
[ ] Discard and restart

Revision notes:

Teacher verification needed:
```

<TakeawayStrip tone="teacher">
  The checklist is not the goal. The goal is a teacher-approved version that is safer, clearer, and more teachable than the AI draft.
</TakeawayStrip>
