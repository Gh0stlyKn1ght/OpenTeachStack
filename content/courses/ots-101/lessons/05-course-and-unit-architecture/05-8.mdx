# Chapter Checkpoint

## Core Idea

Course and Unit Architecture is about building coherent units where lessons, activities, assessments, and revision notes support one another. In this section, **Chapter Checkpoint** turns that larger chapter focus into one concrete teacher move.

This section stands on its own inside the OTS-101 course book and does not depend on a reused standalone lesson body.

The work here should help the teacher decide what belongs in the unit sequence and what should stay optional, archived, or outside the scope. It should also leave behind evidence that can be reused in the chapter artifact: **Mini-unit map and lesson template**.

## Do This

- Name the immediate teacher problem connected to Chapter Checkpoint.
- Use this section to pause and review the chapter artifact.
- Connect the work to one real classroom context: a course, unit, lesson, assessment, resource, or delivery routine.
- Record the decision you made about what belongs in the unit sequence and what should stay optional, archived, or outside the scope.
- Save the result where it can support the chapter artifact: Mini-unit map and lesson template.

## Evidence of Completion

- a checkpoint note with at least one revision decision exists for Chapter Checkpoint.
- The work names the classroom context and the teacher decision it supports.
- The evidence connects to Mini-unit map and lesson template.
- Any AI-assisted language has been reviewed, revised, or rejected by the teacher.
- A short revision note explains what should change before reuse.

## Verification Check

- Do not include student-identifiable information, private account details, hidden assessment keys, or private school files.
- Check for the chapter risk: collecting activities without a visible learning path.
- the revision decision is specific enough to act on
- Use official sources or local policy when software behavior, licensing, safety, or standards interpretation matters.
- Keep the artifact small enough that another teacher could review it without hidden context.
