# Assignments, Labs, Rubrics, and Feedback

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

A lesson gives students the path.

An assignment or lab gives students the work.

A rubric names what quality looks like.

Feedback helps students improve after they try.

## What Teachers Should Notice

The chapter is trying to make one teacher decision visible before any AI draft gets treated as finished.

Those pieces need to point at the same learning target. If they do not, students can complete the activity and still fail to show the learning you meant to teach.

## Why this chapter matters

AI can draft assignments very quickly.

That is useful, but it can also produce activities that are busy, broad, or disconnected from the actual learning target.

A worksheet can look complete without measuring anything important.

A lab can feel hands-on without producing usable evidence.

## The Course-Building Move

This is where the teacher turns vague course work into something students, colleagues, and future revisions can actually inspect.

A rubric can sound official while scoring effort, neatness, or vague quality words instead of the skill students were supposed to practice.

This chapter keeps the chain tight.

## What you will build

By the end of this chapter, you will have an assignment and feedback packet.

It will include:

- one assignment or lab task
- student-facing task directions
- evidence students will submit or show
- a small rubric tied to the target
- feedback language students can use for revision
- teacher verification notes

## The chapter path

1. First, we separate activity from evidence.
2. Then we design assignment and lab tasks that prove the target.
3. Then we build a small rubric with observable criteria.
4. Then we write feedback that helps students revise.
5. Finally, we check whether the packet actually measures the learning target.

<TakeawayStrip tone="teacher">
  The goal is not more grading paperwork. The goal is clearer evidence of learning.
</TakeawayStrip>

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter gives teachers one usable way to handle assignments labs rubrics feedback without asking AI to invent the whole course. The point is to name the teaching decision first, then use AI only where it can support that decision.

## Lessons in This Chapter

- Start by naming what the teacher is actually building.
- Separate the learning plan from the student-facing material.
- Use AI for draft support, examples, and cleanup only after the teacher has named the target.
- Save one visible artifact so the chapter ends with evidence, not just reading.

## Exit Criteria

By the end of this chapter, the teacher should be able to point to Assignment and feedback packet and explain what changed because of the chapter, what still needs review, and what is safe to reuse with students.
