> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

A rubric should make quality visible.

It should not just make grading look official.

## Use the Model Slowly

A framework helps only when the teacher pauses long enough to label each part.

For this course, keep rubrics small. Three criteria are usually enough for one assignment or lab.

## What makes a rubric useful?

A useful rubric names qualities the teacher can actually see in the student work.

Weak criterion:

> Shows good understanding.

Stronger criterion:

> Explains why the selected evidence supports the claim.

The stronger criterion is observable. The teacher can point to the explanation and decide whether it is there.

## Avoid vague quality words

Rubrics often hide behind words like:

- excellent
- thoughtful
- complete
- creative
- strong
- weak
- good effort
- demonstrates understanding

These words may be useful as summaries, but they need observable details.

Instead of:

```text
Excellent explanation
```

write:

```text
Explanation names the chosen source, gives two credibility clues, and explains how those clues support the choice.
```

## Use three criteria

For a short assignment, try this structure:

| Criterion | What the teacher looks for |
|---|---|
| Target skill | Does the work show the skill named in the learning target? |
| Evidence | Does the student use required source details, examples, data, or reasoning? |
| Clarity | Can the reader understand the student's decision or product? |

You can adjust the labels, but keep the rubric tied to visible evidence.

## Write levels only if you need them

Sometimes a checklist is enough.

- For early drafts, use a short scale that students can remember.

```text
Meets target / Needs revision
```

- For larger assignments, use the full rubric only when each row changes the feedback.

```text
Yes / Not yet
```

A giant four-level rubric is not automatically better.

If the assignment is small, the rubric should be small too.

## Ask AI for rubric help carefully

Use your prompt pattern:

```text
Using this learning target and assignment directions, draft a three-criterion rubric. Each criterion must describe observable evidence in student work. Avoid vague words like good, excellent, thoughtful, or complete unless they are explained with visible details.
```

Then revise the rubric yourself.

AI can suggest language, but the teacher decides what counts.

</TakeawayStrip>

## Classroom Scenario

A teacher asks AI for a rubric for a source-credibility assignment. The draft includes rows for effort, grammar, creativity, and completion.

Those rows may be easy to score, but they do not show whether students can judge a source using evidence.

## Weak Version

Weak rubric criterion:

> Shows excellent understanding of source credibility.

That sounds official, but two teachers could score it differently because the visible evidence is not named.

## Better Version

Better rubric criterion:

> Names two credibility clues from the source and explains how those clues support the trust decision.

Now the criterion points to evidence in the student work.

## Build Step

Write three rubric criteria for one assignment:

```text
Target skill criterion:
Evidence criterion:
Clarity or revision criterion:
Words I will avoid because they are too vague:
```

## Quality Check

A rubric row is ready when a student can use it before submitting and a teacher can use it without guessing what the phrase means.

## Safety / Accessibility / Source Note

Rubrics can create access barriers when they reward hidden expectations. If the criterion depends on format, language load, speed, or presentation style, make sure that requirement belongs to the target.

## Reflection

Which word in your rubric sounds clear to you but might be vague to a student: excellent, complete, thoughtful, creative, detailed, or something else?

## Capstone Connection

Save the small rubric with the assignment packet. The capstone should show that quality criteria match the target, not just that a rubric exists.

## How to Use It

Use the rubric framework after the assignment directions are clear. First, underline the evidence students will produce. Then write criteria that point directly at that evidence.

Do not ask AI for a full rubric until you have named the target skill, the required evidence, and the qualities students can actually revise.
