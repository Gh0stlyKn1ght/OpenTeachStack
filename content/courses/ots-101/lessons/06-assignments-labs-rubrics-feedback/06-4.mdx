# Build Artifact: Assignment and Feedback Packet

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

In this lesson, you will build an assignment and feedback packet.

This packet should be small enough to use, but complete enough that students know what to do and teachers know what evidence to review.

## What the packet includes

Your packet needs five parts:

- learning target
- student-facing assignment or lab directions
- evidence students submit or show
- small rubric or success criteria
- feedback prompts for revision
- teacher verification notes

## Step 1: Copy the learning target

```text
Learning target:
I can...
```

Keep the target visible while you design every other part.

## Step 2: Write the assignment or lab directions

Use student-facing language.

```text
Use:

Do:

Make:

Check:
```

Example:

```text
Use the two source links provided. Compare the author, date, evidence, and purpose of each source. Choose the source you would trust more for a class project. Write four sentences explaining your choice with two credibility clues. Before submitting, check that you named the source, included two clues, and explained why they matter.
```

## Step 3: Name the evidence

```text
Student evidence:
Students submit a four-sentence explanation that names the chosen source, includes two credibility clues, and explains why those clues support the decision.
```

If you cannot name the evidence, the assignment is not ready.

## Step 4: Build the rubric

Keep it small.

| Criterion | Meets target | Needs revision |
|---|---|---|
| Source choice | Names one source and makes a clear trust decision. | Does not clearly choose a source. |
| Evidence | Uses two credibility clues from the sources. | Uses fewer than two clues or gives vague details. |
| Explanation | Explains why the clues support the choice. | States an opinion without explaining the evidence. |

Revise the criteria to match your own lesson.

## Step 5: Add feedback prompts

Feedback should help students take a next step.

Use sentence starters like:

- Your strongest evidence is...
- Add one source detail that shows...
- Explain why this clue matters because...
- Your decision is clear, but your evidence needs...
- Before revising, check whether...

These prompts can be used by the teacher, peers, or students during self-review.

## Step 6: Add teacher verification notes

```text
Teacher verification notes:
- Check that links work and are appropriate.
- Confirm source details are accurate.
- Confirm the rubric measures the learning target.
- Check accessibility of source materials.
- Confirm submission instructions match the platform.
```

  A good packet does not need to be huge. It needs to make the task, evidence, quality, and feedback visible.

## Build Step

Read your own assignment as a student would and produce the evidence yourself. If your sample answer does not clearly show the learning target, the task is what needs revision, not the rubric.

## Quality Check

The packet is ready when the rubric scores the same skill the assignment asks for and the target names. If those three drift apart, students can earn a grade without ever showing the learning.

## Capstone Connection

Keep the assignment, rubric, and feedback prompts together in the capstone packet. The capstone should show that your evidence and your criteria measure the same target.
