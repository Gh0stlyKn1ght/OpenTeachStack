# Slides as Delivery Tools

## Why This Matters

A teacher building a first reusable mini-unit needs more than a tool list or a checklist. The goal in **Google Workspace Planning Systems** is to make a classroom system that can be used, inspected, revised, and handed to another educator without needing a meeting first.

For **Slides as Delivery Tools**, the practical pressure is simple: accepting a polished lesson draft before the sources, student task, assessment evidence, and classroom routine are inspectable. This page turns that pressure into a visible update to **Workspace planning map**.

## Classroom Scenario

Use this situation as the working example: an ultrasonic sensor mini-unit where students collect readings, explain uncertainty, revise a threshold, and submit a short design reflection. By the end, the work should support this habit: source-backed planning, plain-language decisions, and visible revision notes.

In this scenario, the learner should be able to point to:

- what the teacher prepares before students see the work,
- what students will read, build, submit, or discuss,
- what evidence shows the work is ready,
- what gets revised after the first use.

## Key Idea

A workspace system works when another teacher can find current files, understand ownership, close the loop after class, and avoid exposing private materials.

Watch for this mistake: Leaving the newest copy, answer key, and student responses mixed together in a shared folder.
