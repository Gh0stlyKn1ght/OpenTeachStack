# Safety, Accessibility, Copyright, and Source Quality

> OTS-101 frame: AI supports teacher-approved course content that becomes student-facing only after review.

A course artifact can be clear, aligned, and organized and still not be ready for students.

Before publishing, teachers need to ask four practical questions:

- Is this safe for students?
- Can students access and use it?
- Are sources and media used responsibly?
- Is the information trustworthy enough for the task?

This chapter turns those questions into a reusable checklist.

<FrameworkBlock title="Publish-readiness guardrails" eyebrow="Teacher checks">
  <ConceptCard title="Safety">
    The content avoids exposing private data, harmful instructions, inappropriate links, or unmanaged risk.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Accessibility">
    Students can read, navigate, understand, and complete the work with reasonable supports.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Copyright">
    Materials, media, excerpts, and reuse choices are appropriate for the classroom context.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Source quality">
    Facts, examples, links, and references are trustworthy enough for students to rely on.
  </ConceptCard>
</FrameworkBlock>

## Why this chapter matters

AI can make a draft look clean without making it safe.

It might suggest an inaccessible activity, summarize a source inaccurately, invent a citation, include copyrighted media without context, or create a task that asks students to share more personal information than necessary.

The teacher needs a practical review routine.

Not panic.

## What Teachers Should Notice

The chapter is trying to make one teacher decision visible before any AI draft gets treated as finished.

Not perfection.

A routine.

## What you will build

By the end of this chapter, you will have a course content safety and access checklist.

It will help you check:

- private student data
- sensitive classroom details
- age-appropriate links and materials
- accessibility of directions, media, and files
- copyright and reuse concerns
- source credibility and accuracy
- teacher verification needs

## The chapter path

1. First, we identify safety risks in AI-assisted course content.
2. Then we make accessibility concrete and teacher-manageable.
3. Then we check copyright and responsible reuse.
4. Then you build a safety and access checklist.
5. Finally, you decide whether the content is ready, needs revision, or should not be published.

<TakeawayStrip tone="teacher">
  Safety and access checks are not extra decorations. They are part of making course content teachable.
</TakeawayStrip>

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter gives teachers one usable way to handle safety accessibility copyright source quality without asking AI to invent the whole course. The point is to name the teaching decision first, then use AI only where it can support that decision.

## Lessons in This Chapter

- Start by naming what the teacher is actually building.
- Separate the learning plan from the student-facing material.
- Use AI for draft support, examples, and cleanup only after the teacher has named the target.
- Save one visible artifact so the chapter ends with evidence, not just reading.

## Exit Criteria

By the end of this chapter, the teacher should be able to point to Course content safety and access checklist and explain what changed because of the chapter, what still needs review, and what is safe to reuse with students.
