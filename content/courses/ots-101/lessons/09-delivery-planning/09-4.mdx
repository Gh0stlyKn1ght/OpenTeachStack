# Videos, Images, and Embedded Resources

## Why This Matters

A teacher building a first reusable mini-unit needs more than a tool list or a checklist. The goal in **Delivery Planning** is to make a classroom system that can be used, inspected, revised, and handed to another educator without needing a meeting first.

For **Videos, Images, and Embedded Resources**, the practical pressure is simple: accepting a polished lesson draft before the sources, student task, assessment evidence, and classroom routine are inspectable. This page turns that pressure into a visible update to **Delivery plan**.

## Classroom Scenario

Use this situation as the working example: an ultrasonic sensor mini-unit where students collect readings, explain uncertainty, revise a threshold, and submit a short design reflection. By the end, the work should support this habit: source-backed planning, plain-language decisions, and visible revision notes.

In this scenario, the learner should be able to point to:

- what the teacher prepares before students see the work,
- what students will read, build, submit, or discuss,
- what evidence shows the work is ready,
- what gets revised after the first use.

## Key Idea

Open work needs reuse instructions, source notes, license decisions, exclusions, and a way for another educator to suggest improvements safely.

Watch for this mistake: Treating a public link as permission to copy or treating a repository as self-explanatory.

- Before class, connect videos images and embedded resources to one setup decision in the delivery plan: what the teacher prepares, where it lives, and which source or safety check keeps it honest.

- After class, review videos images and embedded resources through the delivery planning lens: what evidence proves the move worked, which mistake to avoid (treating a public link as permission to copy or treating a repository as self-explanatory), and what revision supports source-backed planning, plain-language decisions, and visible revision notes.

A strong **videos images and embedded resources** entry is concrete enough that another teacher can inspect it. It names the class context, the artifact location, the student product, the source or safety check, and the revision trigger.
