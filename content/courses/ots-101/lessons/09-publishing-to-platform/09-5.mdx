A platform copy is ready when students can find it, use it, and submit the work without the teacher secretly relying on missing context.

This checkpoint gives you a final release decision.

<FrameworkBlock title="Platform release decision" eyebrow="Checkpoint">
  <ConceptCard title="Ready">
    The platform copy preserves the approved lesson and works from the student view.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Revise">
    The content is close, but formatting, access, submission, or settings need repair.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Do not release">
    Students cannot safely or clearly use the platform copy yet.
  </ConceptCard>
</FrameworkBlock>

## Choose ready when...

Choose ready when:

- the copy came from the approved source
- student view works
- links and files open
- headings and lists are readable
- submission settings match directions
- rubric or success criteria are visible
- safety and accessibility checks still hold
- the platform copy has a source or export note

Ready means the platform version is teachable.

## Choose revise when...

Choose revise when:

- bullets, headings, or tables need cleanup
- a link permission needs fixing
- a file name is confusing
- submission settings do not match directions
- the rubric is hidden
- an example is missing
- the platform copy needs a source note

These are fixable platform problems.

## Choose do not release when...

Choose do not release when:

- students cannot access required materials
- the wrong version was copied
- private or unsafe content is visible
- important claims are unverified
- the task cannot be submitted as written
- the page depends on teacher explanation that is not present
- nobody can tell whether the platform copy is current

Do not release protects students from a broken learning path.

## Final release note

Write one short note:

```text
Platform release decision: Ready / Revise / Do not release
Reason:
Required fix:
Source/export note:
```

Example:

```text
Platform release decision: Revise
Reason: The lesson content is correct, but students cannot open the source document.
Required fix: Change file sharing to class access and test student view again.
Source/export note: Exported from lesson-05-student-page-approved.md on 2026-06-26.
```

## What to keep for the capstone

Save the checklist and release note.

Your final mini-course packet should not only include course content. It should show that the content can be published to a student-facing platform without losing clarity, access, or source control.

<TakeawayStrip tone="teacher">
  The platform is not the finish line until the student-facing version actually works for students.
</TakeawayStrip>

## Evidence to keep

Save the release note as evidence that the student view was checked, not assumed. Review again when the platform changes, the source lesson is revised, or a student reports something broken.
