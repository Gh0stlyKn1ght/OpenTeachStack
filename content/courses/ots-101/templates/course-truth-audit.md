# Course Truth Audit

## Purpose

Use this before treating an AI-generated course package, LMS module, website section, Drive folder, or Teachable export as a real course.

The audit checks whether the course has a source of truth or only a clean container.

## When to use it

Use it when AI has produced course folders, outlines, quiz plans, download lists, platform pages, or final-project notes and you need to decide what is actually ready.

## Completed fictional example

Course: Intro to Robotics Safety  
Mini-unit: Safe robot startup and shutdown  
Platform target: Google Classroom

| Course truth check | Evidence found | Status | Next action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Learning goal | Students explain and perform the four-step startup routine. | present | Keep. |
| Lesson sequence | Outline lists startup, shutdown, and emergency stop. | weak | Turn outline into two student-facing lesson pages. |
| Student-facing lesson body | No lesson page exists yet. | missing | Draft the first lesson before publishing. |
| Worked example | No completed startup checklist example. | missing | Create a fictional completed checklist. |
| Weak or risky example | No non-example. | missing | Add a checklist with unsafe skipped steps and explain the problem. |
| Practice task | Students complete a startup/shutdown check. | weak | Name what students submit. |
| Assessment evidence | Quiz plan exists, but items do not map to the target. | weak | Align each item to the learning target. |
| Safety check | Teacher must verify against approved robotics safety routine. | present | Verify before students use it. |
| Revision note | No review date. | missing | Add review after the first lab day. |
| Export package or platform location | Google Classroom topic exists. | present | Do not publish until lesson and evidence are ready. |

## Quality check

- The audit separates source course content from platform export files.
- Missing items are allowed to stay missing instead of being invented.
- The next action names one specific thing to draft, verify, revise, or archive.
- The teacher can see whether the course is ready, draft, or only packaged.

## Safety note

Do not paste private course folders, student work, grades, accommodations, internal policy, answer keys, or equipment-specific safety procedures into AI tools. Use fictional examples or sanitized summaries when testing prompts.
