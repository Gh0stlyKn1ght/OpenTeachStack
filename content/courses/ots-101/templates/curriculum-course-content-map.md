# Curriculum vs Course Content Map

## Purpose

Use this to prevent AI from mixing learning-plan decisions with student-facing materials.

## When to use it

Use it before drafting a lesson, assignment, module page, or LMS unit.

Use it before building export packages for Teachable, Canvas, Google Classroom, or a course website.

## Completed fictional example

| Course piece | Curriculum decision | Student-facing content | Instructional material | Platform location | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Safe startup routine | Students can perform the four-step routine before powering a robot. | Lesson page and checklist | Demo notes and teacher setup list | Google Classroom lesson post | AI may revise wording, not safety sequence. |
| Teachable upload package | No new curriculum decision. It only packages the mini-unit. | Links to the finished lesson and checklist | Instructor talking points | Teachable draft course | Export after the source lesson, task, and assessment are ready. |

## Quality check

- The curriculum decision is observable.
- The student-facing content is something students actually use.
- Teacher-only material is not mixed into the student view.
- Platform or export rows point back to source course content.

## Safety note

When safety routines are involved, verify the sequence against local policy and approved equipment guidance before students use it.
