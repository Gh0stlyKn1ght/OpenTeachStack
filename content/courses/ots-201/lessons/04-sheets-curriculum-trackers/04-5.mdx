# Checkpoint Review

## Why This Matters

The final checkpoint is where audit quality becomes practical:
could a colleague run this unit if the owner is absent?

## Core Idea

Review decisions in three layers:

- Functionality: Can the lesson be launched from the tracker?
- Integrity: Are evidence and blockers explicit?
- Maintainability: Can next steps be assigned clearly?

## Review Protocol

Use the table below for one full review cycle:

| Area | Review question | Pass condition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Function | What is the active lesson for this week? | One row marked `Active` with valid links |
| Integrity | What evidence will students submit? | One concrete destination and expected format per lesson |
| Maintainability | Who edits next? | One owner and next-review date per row |

## Decision Task

Create a checkpoint verdict row in the tracker:

| Lesson focus | Checkpoint status | Why | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Curriculum tracker system | Ready | Links and status rules are consistent for active lessons | Maya |

If any lesson is partially ready:

- label as `Revise`
- specify the missing field
- set a dated follow-up

## Quality Check

Simulate a substitute opening the sheet and teaching `Distance launch`.
They should complete:

1. Open active lesson row
2. Use student Doc and deck links
3. Direct students to evidence row
4. Update post-teaching status to Review needed or Ready

If any step fails, do not mark chapter complete.

## Reflection

What is one blocker that still relies on a team message rather than the sheet? Move that blocker into `Blocker` with a date and owner.
