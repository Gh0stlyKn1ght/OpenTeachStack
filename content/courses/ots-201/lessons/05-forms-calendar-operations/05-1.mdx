# Forms as Checks for Understanding

## Why This Matters

A teacher team making Google Workspace reliable for weekly planning needs more than a tool list or a checklist. The goal in **Forms and Calendar for Classroom Operations** is to make a classroom system that can be used, inspected, revised, and handed to another educator without needing a meeting first.

For **Forms as Checks for Understanding**, the practical pressure is simple: building one more folder or tracker without deciding who updates it, when it closes, and how old versions leave the active workspace. This page turns that pressure into a visible update to **Operations workflow map**.

## Classroom Scenario

Use this situation as the working example: a ninth grade technology team maintaining a current-unit folder, shared tracker, response sheet, delivery deck, and command center. By the end, the work should support this habit: clear ownership, predictable file locations, and closeout routines.

In this scenario, the learner should be able to point to:

- what the teacher prepares before students see the work,
- what students will read, build, submit, or discuss,
- what evidence shows the work is ready,
- what gets revised after the first use.

## Key Idea

A workspace system works when another teacher can find current files, understand ownership, close the loop after class, and avoid exposing private materials.

Watch for this mistake: Leaving the newest copy, answer key, and student responses mixed together in a shared folder.

## What You Will Do

Make this page concrete by writing three small decisions:

- Before class, connect forms as checks for understanding to one setup decision in the operations workflow map: what the teacher prepares, where it lives, and which source or safety check keeps it honest.
- During class, connect forms as checks for understanding to one student action in shared drive, docs, slides, forms, sheets, and calendar routines: what learners read, build, submit, discuss, or revise while working through a ninth grade technology team maintaining a current-unit folder, shared tracker, response sheet, delivery deck, and command center.
- After class, review forms as checks for understanding through the forms and calendar for classroom operations lens: what evidence proves the move worked, which mistake to avoid (leaving the newest copy, answer key, and student responses mixed together in a shared folder), and what revision supports clear ownership, predictable file locations, and closeout routines.
