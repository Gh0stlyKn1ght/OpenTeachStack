# Forms as Intake and Reflection

## Why This Matters

Intake and reflection Forms can help a teacher prepare, but they can also create unnecessary data clutter. The difference is whether each question has a clear use.

Before asking students for information, decide what you will do with the answer, where the response will live, and whether the question is appropriate to collect in a class workflow.

A workspace system works when intake and reflection data are useful, limited, reviewed, and closed out.

## Core Idea

Ask only for information that supports a visible teacher action.

For this course, use a simple privacy filter:

| Question type | Usually appropriate? | Teacher check |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Project choice, material need, group progress | Yes | Does it help prepare instruction or logistics? |
| Confidence rating or reflection on strategy | Yes | Is it framed for learning, not judgment? |
| Personal situation, sensitive identity, private family details | Usually no | Is there a safer school-approved channel? |
| Medical, discipline, or emergency information | No for this workflow | Use official school systems and policies. |

## Classroom Example

Maya wants to prepare robotics project materials before build day. She needs to know which sensor path each team plans to test and whether they are missing a kit part.

Weak intake question:

> Tell me anything I should know before build day.

That question is too broad. It invites private details and produces answers that are hard to act on.

Better intake questions:

| Form question | Follow-up action |
| --- | --- |
| Which sensor challenge is your team choosing first? | Prepare the right station labels and sample code card. |
| Which material do you need checked before class starts? | Put supply checks into the first five minutes. |
| What is one test your team must run before changing code? | Identify teams that need test-plan support. |

Reflection questions after build day:

| Form question | Follow-up action |
| --- | --- |
| What evidence did your team collect today? | Check whether teams have enough evidence for the next milestone. |
| What is one decision your team changed because of data? | Find examples to discuss in the next class. |
| What is your next test? | Group teams by next support need. |

## Verification Checklist

- Each question has a clear use.
- The Form avoids unnecessary private information.
- The response Sheet is named before responses arrive.
- The review window happens before the information expires.
- The follow-up action is something the teacher can actually do.
- The Form has a closeout rule.
