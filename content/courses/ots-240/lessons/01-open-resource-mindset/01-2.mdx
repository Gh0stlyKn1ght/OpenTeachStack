# License and Terms Decisions

## Why This Matters

Open work needs when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Open Resource Mindset**, the work sits inside open curriculum publishing with GitHub, README files, licenses, attribution, and review routines. The risk is treating free-to-view material as automatically open, reusable, remixable, or safe to publish. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Open resource decision log**.

## Core Idea

Treat this as a boundary lesson. Name what should be shared, what should stay private, what must be checked, and who is responsible for the final decision.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the license terms decisions decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

In 01.2, focus on license terms decisions. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a repository file, README section, issue, pull request, source note, or license entry. Connect it to open resource decision log, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect license terms decisions back to open resource mindset. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.
