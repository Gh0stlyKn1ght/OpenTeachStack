# Setup, Use, and License Notes

## Why This Matters

Open work needs when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Writing Useful READMEs**, the work sits inside open curriculum publishing with GitHub, README files, licenses, attribution, and review routines. The risk is treating free-to-view material as automatically open, reusable, remixable, or safe to publish. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Course README**.

## Core Idea

Treat this as a boundary lesson. Name what should be shared, what should stay private, what must be checked, and who is responsible for the final decision.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the setup use license notes decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

Connect setup use license notes back to writing useful readmes. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.
