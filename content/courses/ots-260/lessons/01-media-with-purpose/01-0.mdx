# Chapter Plan

## Why This Matters

Teachers often add media late, which is why it feels decorative and unconnected.
This chapter starts by deciding when each media type is needed and what it must prove.

## Core Idea

Every asset is accepted only if it serves one learning objective clearly:

- explain a process,
- reduce cognitive load,
- support accessibility,
- or make review and rehearsal faster.

## Chapter Goal

Build a **Media purpose map** with five rows:
- objective
- chosen asset
- why this asset
- who may use it
- evidence of accessibility and safety

## Classroom Scenario

Topic: robotics safety check routine.

You want to teach:
- what to check first,
- what tools are required,
- where students commonly confuse sequence.

You plan to use:
1) one labeled diagram,
2) one quick slide with timing cues,
3) one short audio cue checklist.

## Activity: Create the Map

Create this starter table in your note file:

| Objective | Asset | Why this supports learning | Format | Accessibility pass |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Understand sequence order | flow diagram | shows dependency clearly | image | alt text + contrast check |
| Recall tool list | slide | compact pacing support | slide | readable fonts + short bullet |
| Reduce panic in labs | audio cue list | supports multiple learning styles | text/audio | plain language |

## Plan Completion Rule

Do not move to 01.1 until each row includes:
- one owner,
- one next review date,
- one acceptance condition.
