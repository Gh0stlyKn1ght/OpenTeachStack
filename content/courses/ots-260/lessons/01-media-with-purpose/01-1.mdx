# Media as Explanation, Not Decoration

## Why This Matters

A teacher using media to improve lesson delivery needs more than a tool list or a checklist. The goal in **Media With Purpose** is to make a classroom system that can be used, inspected, revised, and handed to another educator without needing a meeting first.

For **Media as Explanation, Not Decoration**, the practical pressure is simple: adding attractive media that distracts, invents details, hides important information, or cannot be used by every student. This page turns that pressure into a visible update to **Media purpose map**.

## Classroom Scenario

Use this situation as the working example: a sensor lesson media packet with one diagram, one short demonstration clip plan, alt text, captions, and a teacher delivery cue. By the end, the work should support this habit: use media only when it clarifies a learning move and includes an accessible alternative.

In this scenario, the learner should be able to point to:

- what the teacher prepares before students see the work,
- what students will read, build, submit, or discuss,
- what evidence shows the work is ready,
- what gets revised after the first use.

## Key Idea

Media belongs in a lesson when it clarifies a learning move and remains usable without sight, sound, perfect bandwidth, or a specific platform.

Watch for this mistake: Using a dramatic image or video that looks polished but does not explain the concept or support accessibility.

## What You Will Do

Make this page concrete by writing three small decisions:

- Before class, connect media as explanation not decoration to one setup decision in the media purpose map: what the teacher prepares, where it lives, and which source or safety check keeps it honest.
- During class, connect media as explanation not decoration to one student action in slides, diagrams, short videos, image prompts, captions, transcripts, and accessibility alternatives: what learners read, build, submit, discuss, or revise while working through a sensor lesson media packet with one diagram, one short demonstration clip plan, alt text, captions, and a teacher delivery cue.
- After class, review media as explanation not decoration through the media with purpose lens: what evidence proves the move worked, which mistake to avoid (using a dramatic image or video that looks polished but does not explain the concept or support accessibility), and what revision supports use media only when it clarifies a learning move and includes an accessible alternative.

## What Good Work Looks Like

A strong **media as explanation not decoration** entry is concrete enough that another teacher can inspect it. It names the class context, the artifact location, the student product, the source or safety check, and the revision trigger.

For this lesson, a strong entry might say:

> Media entry: Diagram shows sensor-to-threshold flow; alt text names the sequence; transcript covers the 90-second demo clip.

That level of detail is enough to review. It is also small enough to revise after one classroom use.
