# Chapter Plan

## Why This Matters

Media belongs in a lesson when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **AI Image Workflows**, the work sits inside instructional media, AI image review, diagrams, slides, captions, transcripts, and accessibility checks. The risk is adding attractive media that distracts from the learning target, invents details, hides important information, or excludes students. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Image prompt and review log**.

## Core Idea

Map the chapter before building. Choose one classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an image, diagram, slide, short video, transcript, caption file, or media review row. Then name the classroom purpose it serves and the evidence that will show whether the work is ready.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is the opening plan for image workflows, the teacher names the sequence before creating files or artifacts. The plan separates what will be learned, what will be built, and what evidence will prove that the build is ready.

In 02.0, focus on chapter planning decisions. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an image, diagram, slide, short video, transcript, caption file, or media review row. Connect it to image prompt log, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect chapter planning decisions back to image workflows. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: the media makes the lesson easier to understand and can be used by students who need text, audio, visual, or pacing support.

A reviewer should be able to find the chapter planning decisions decision, inspect the image prompt log evidence, and understand how it supports image workflows.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
