# Slide Roles in a Lesson

## Why This Matters

A slide sequence should reduce ambiguity: students should know whether to read, listen, do, or reflect on each screen.

## Core Idea

Every slide must have one primary role and one expected student action.

- **Orient**: what students should expect.
- **Teach**: one concept, one takeaway.
- **Do**: explicit task with criteria.
- **Check**: quick evidence of understanding.
- **Close**: recap and next step.

## Activity: Role Mapping

Build a role map for your draft deck and align each slide before adding visual polish.

| Slide | Role | Expected action | Why this role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [#] | Orient / Teach / Do / Check / Close | [student action] | [teaching reason] |

## Practice

Open `Delivery deck` and record five slides with explicit roles:
- 1 orient
- 2 teach
- 1 do
- 1 check

Mark each row with owner and status.

## Common Mistake

Mixing two roles on one slide (for example, teach + check) without clear cues. Split or simplify.
