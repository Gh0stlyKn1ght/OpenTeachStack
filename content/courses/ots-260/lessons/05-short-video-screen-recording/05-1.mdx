# When Video Helps

## Why This Matters

Media belongs in a lesson when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Short Video and Screen Recording**, the work sits inside instructional media, AI image review, diagrams, slides, captions, transcripts, and accessibility checks. The risk is adding attractive media that distracts from the learning target, invents details, hides important information, or excludes students. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Short video plan**.

## Core Idea

Connect the media choice to the learning task. Record what the asset explains, how students will use it, and what accessibility support it needs.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the when video helps decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

In 05.1, focus on when video helps. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an image, diagram, slide, short video, transcript, caption file, or media review row. Connect it to short video, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect when video helps back to short video screen recording. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: the media makes the lesson easier to understand and can be used by students who need text, audio, visual, or pacing support.

A reviewer should be able to find the when video helps decision, inspect the short video evidence, and understand how it supports short video screen recording.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
