# Short Video Plan

## Why This Matters

Media belongs in a lesson when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Short Video and Screen Recording**, the work sits inside instructional media, AI image review, diagrams, slides, captions, transcripts, and accessibility checks. The risk is adding attractive media that distracts from the learning target, invents details, hides important information, or excludes students. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Short video plan**.

## Core Idea

Build the **Short video plan** as a usable artifact, not just a note. The finished version shows the decision, the classroom context, the evidence, and the next maintenance step.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is an artifact lesson, the teacher turns short video into something another educator can use. The artifact needs labels, examples, status, review notes, and a next action instead of a vague promise to improve later.

In 05.3, focus on short video. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an image, diagram, slide, short video, transcript, caption file, or media review row. Connect it to short video, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect short video back to short video screen recording. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Classroom Example

Use this working situation: a teacher is building a media packet with one explanatory diagram, one delivery deck, one short video plan, and accessibility notes for each asset.

## Build The Work

Update the **Short video plan** with one row or section for **Short Video Plan**.

| Field | Your note |
| --- | --- |
| Lesson focus | short video |
| Classroom context | Where short video screen recording appears in your course, unit, tool, site, media packet, safety routine, or capstone |
| Working material | Classroom material or teacher decision: an image, diagram, slide, short video, transcript, caption file, or media review row |
| Teacher decision | What short video causes you to keep, revise, test, publish, restrict, or remove |
| Artifact status | What proves short video is ready or what blocks it |
| Maintenance trigger | When the short video decision should be checked again |

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: the media makes the lesson easier to understand and can be used by students who need text, audio, visual, or pacing support.

A reviewer should be able to find the short video decision, inspect the short video evidence, and understand how it supports short video screen recording.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
