# Peer Review and Revision

## Why This Matters

Media belongs in a lesson when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Accessible Lesson Media Packet**, the work sits inside instructional media, AI image review, diagrams, slides, captions, transcripts, and accessibility checks. The risk is adding attractive media that distracts from the learning target, invents details, hides important information, or excludes students. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Accessible lesson media packet**.

## Core Idea

Make the decision readable to someone else. The artifact explains the purpose, the source of truth, the review routine, and how future revisions will be recorded.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the peer revision decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

In 06.2, focus on peer revision. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an image, diagram, slide, short video, transcript, caption file, or media review row. Connect it to accessible lesson media packet, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect peer revision back to accessible lesson media packet. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Classroom Example

Use this working situation: a teacher is building a media packet with one explanatory diagram, one delivery deck, one short video plan, and accessibility notes for each asset.

For **Peer Review and Revision**, choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an image, diagram, slide, short video, transcript, caption file, or media review row. Keep the example small enough to review during the lesson and focused on peer revision.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: the media makes the lesson easier to understand and can be used by students who need text, audio, visual, or pacing support.

A reviewer should be able to find the peer revision decision, inspect the accessible lesson media packet evidence, and understand how it supports accessible lesson media packet.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
