# The Account Inventory

## Core Idea

Before security habits work, we need visibility.  
This section is about building a simple inventory of the accounts and services you actually use, and identifying where one weak point could create the most damage.

## Do This

- Create an account inventory with four columns: `account type`, `why used`, `last reviewed`, `risk if compromised`.
- Add at least one item from each category: teaching platform, communication, personal, and publishing.
- Mark which entries are critical for school continuity versus convenient-only.
- Pick one “critical + neglected” item and identify the first secure action you can take this week.

## Evidence of Completion

- A table or list with at least one row from each requested account type category.
- One item marked `critical` and one marked `low risk`.
- A short follow-up date for your first action.
- Notes prepared for conversion into the chapter artifact without exposing secrets.

## Safety Check

- Do not include passwords, codes, or full account usernames in the shareable artifact.
- If you use real examples, remove names of schools, students, and personal email addresses.
- Escalate any suspected breach or unauthorized access through school channels before continuing.
- Keep an unshared private version with the fuller context.

## Reflection

What did your inventory reveal about which account class deserves your next security action?
