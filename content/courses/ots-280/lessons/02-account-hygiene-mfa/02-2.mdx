# Password Reuse and Weak Recovery

## Core Idea

Password reuse and weak recovery settings create a chain reaction: one leak can open many systems.  
This section helps you reduce damage by separating identity per service and validating recovery choices.

## Do This

- List any recurring password themes and flag shared passwords across two or more non-trusted accounts.
- For your top two critical accounts, verify a valid recovery path without writing full recovery answers.
- Identify the weakest recovery pattern (for example old phone number, inactive recovery email, missing backup method).
- Define one immediate fix and one long-term fix for each top account.

## Evidence of Completion

- Evidence of at least two accounts reviewed for reuse/recovery risk.
- For each reviewed account, one recovery gap is documented and a mitigation step is chosen.
- The chapter artifact is updated with at least one `weak recovery` action.
- A fallback action is identified if immediate reset is blocked.

## Safety Check

- Do not write actual passwords, secrets, or recovery codes anywhere shared.
- Keep recovery data references as placeholders (`recov-email-a`, `recovery-phone`).
- If recovery is impossible after safe checks, escalate to platform support and document who you notified.
- Store the private baseline only in your own secure location.

## Reflection

Which account has the biggest exposure from weak recovery, and what is the first reliable fix?
