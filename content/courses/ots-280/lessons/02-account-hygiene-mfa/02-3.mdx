# Password Managers

## Core Idea

A password manager helps shift security from memory to process.  
The goal is not “stronger password,” it is reducing future failures by making safe behavior easier to repeat.

## Do This

- Compare three options and note how each handles sync, ownership, and recovery.
- Add one account from your inventory and plan one safe migration path for it.
- Record your policy for creating unique passphrases (length, format, and storage style).
- If your workflow allows it, set a rule: no new account starts without manager capture.

## Evidence of Completion

- Three criteria for comparing password-manager options are documented.
- One real account migration step is documented.
- A naming and storage rule is added to your chapter checklist.
- The policy is realistic for your school or personal environment.

## Safety Check

- Do not record live secrets, master password, PINs, or biometric data.
- Do not mention school account credentials in public channels.
- Keep a private note for any service migration exceptions and avoid sharing those exceptions publicly.

## Reflection

What would make your account-management process easier to repeat on a normal workday?
