# Password Manager and MFA Checklist

## Why This Matters

Teacher safety work should reduce exposure when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Account Hygiene and MFA**, the work sits inside teacher digital safety, account hygiene, public profile review, connection safety, and incident response. The risk is turning safety into fear instead of reducing specific exposure with practical routines. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Password manager and MFA checklist**.

## Core Idea

Build the **Password manager and MFA checklist** as a usable artifact, not just a note. The finished version shows the decision, the classroom context, the evidence, and the next maintenance step.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is an artifact lesson, the teacher turns password manager mfa checklist into something another educator can use. The artifact needs labels, examples, status, review notes, and a next action instead of a vague promise to improve later.

## What Not To Record

The checklist should not contain passwords, MFA backup codes, security-question answers, student identifiers, or screenshots that reveal account details. Record the status and next action, not the secret.

## Review Decisions

| Status | Evidence | Next action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ready | Unique password and MFA status are recorded without exposing secrets. | Calendar a semester review. |
| Revise | Account is listed but recovery or MFA status is unclear. | Check settings and update status. |
| Blocked | Account uses shared credentials or cannot be accessed by the rightful owner. | Escalate through district support or account recovery process. |

## Quality Check

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
