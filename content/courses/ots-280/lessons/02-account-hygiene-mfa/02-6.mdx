# Checkpoint Review

## Why This Matters

Teacher safety work should reduce exposure when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Account Hygiene and MFA**, the work sits inside teacher digital safety, account hygiene, public profile review, connection safety, and incident response. The risk is turning safety into fear instead of reducing specific exposure with practical routines. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Password manager and MFA checklist**.

## Core Idea

Run a checkpoint on the **Password manager and MFA checklist**. Mark what is ready, what needs revision, what is blocked, and what evidence supports that decision.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is the checkpoint for account hygiene mfa, the teacher reviews the artifact as a working system. The checkpoint looks for missing decisions, unclear ownership, weak evidence, and maintenance steps that would be forgotten during a busy week.

In 02.6, focus on checkpoint review decisions. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an account, profile, device setting, website form, repository, domain record, or incident note. Connect it to password manager mfa checklist, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect checkpoint review decisions back to account hygiene mfa. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Classroom Example

Use this working situation: a teacher is auditing accounts, public profiles, device settings, repo exposure, website forms, and response steps without collecting private student information.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: the action lowers a named risk, avoids unnecessary data collection, and gives the teacher a calm next step.

A reviewer should be able to find the checkpoint review decisions decision, inspect the password manager mfa checklist evidence, and understand how it supports account hygiene mfa.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
