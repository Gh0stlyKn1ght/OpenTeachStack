# Chapter Plan

## Why This Matters

Teacher safety work should reduce exposure when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Email, Phishing, and Impersonation**, the work sits inside teacher digital safety, account hygiene, public profile review, connection safety, and incident response. The risk is turning safety into fear instead of reducing specific exposure with practical routines. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Phishing response checklist**.

## Core Idea

Map the chapter before building. Choose one classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an account, profile, device setting, website form, repository, domain record, or incident note. Then name the classroom purpose it serves and the evidence that will show whether the work is ready.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is the opening plan for email phishing impersonation, the teacher names the sequence before creating files or artifacts. The plan separates what will be learned, what will be built, and what evidence will prove that the build is ready.

In 04.0, focus on chapter planning decisions.
