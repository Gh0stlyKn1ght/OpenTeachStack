# Auditing Public Profiles

## Why This Matters

Teacher safety work should reduce exposure when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Social Media and Doxxing Risk**, the work sits inside teacher digital safety, account hygiene, public profile review, connection safety, and incident response. The risk is turning safety into fear instead of reducing specific exposure with practical routines. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Public profile audit**.

## Core Idea

Turn the lesson idea into an inspectable decision. Name the classroom use, the evidence, the review step, and the next revision trigger.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the auditing public profiles decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

In 05.2, focus on auditing public profiles. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an account, profile, device setting, website form, repository, domain record, or incident note. Connect it to public profile audit, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect auditing public profiles back to social media doxxing risk. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.
