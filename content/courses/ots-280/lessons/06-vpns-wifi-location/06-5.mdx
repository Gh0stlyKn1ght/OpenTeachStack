# Chapter Checkpoint

## Core Idea

VPNs, Wi-Fi, and Location Privacy is about understanding what public networks, HTTPS, VPNs, and location signals do and do not protect. In this section, **Chapter Checkpoint** turns that chapter focus into one practical safety habit.

The goal is not to make teachers paranoid or technical for its own sake. The goal is to help a teacher decide which teacher tasks should wait for a trusted network or device and leave behind a safe, maintainable artifact: **Connection safety checklist**.

- Do not include student-identifiable information, passwords, recovery codes, MFA backup codes, account secrets, private links, hidden answer keys, or live incident details.
- Check for the chapter risk: treating a VPN as protection from phishing, malware, weak passwords, school policy, or unsafe sharing.
