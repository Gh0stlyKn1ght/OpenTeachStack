# Chapter Checkpoint

## Core Idea

Browser, Device, and File Safety is about cleaning everyday browser, device, download, screenshot, permission, and metadata risks. In this section, **Chapter Checkpoint** turns that chapter focus into one practical safety habit.

The goal is not to make teachers paranoid or technical for its own sake. The goal is to help a teacher decide what to remove, update, lock, inspect, or escalate before publishing more work and leave behind a safe, maintainable artifact: **Device and browser hardening checklist**.

- Record the teacher decision you made about what to remove, update, lock, inspect, or escalate before publishing more work.
- Save the result where it can support the chapter artifact: Device and browser hardening checklist.

## Evidence of Completion

- a checkpoint note exists for Chapter Checkpoint.
- The evidence connects to Device and browser hardening checklist.

- A revision or maintenance note explains what should be checked again later.

## Safety Check

- Do not include student-identifiable information, passwords, recovery codes, MFA backup codes, account secrets, private links, hidden answer keys, or live incident details.
- Check for the chapter risk: uploading private files or student screenshots to random scanners, converters, or AI tools.
- the checkpoint does not publish sensitive account, device, profile, or incident details.
- Escalate to district IT, administration, platform support, or appropriate authorities when the situation exceeds normal teacher maintenance.
- Keep a private version of sensitive notes and share only sanitized summaries for review.
