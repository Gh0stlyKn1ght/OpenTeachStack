# Personal and Student Exposure Risks

## Why This Matters

Teacher safety work should reduce exposure when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Website Safety for Teacher Sites**, the work sits inside teacher digital safety, account hygiene, public profile review, connection safety, and incident response. The risk is turning safety into fear instead of reducing specific exposure with practical routines. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Teacher website security audit**.

## Core Idea

Treat this as a boundary lesson. Name what should be shared, what should stay private, what must be checked, and who is responsible for the final decision.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the personal student exposure risks decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

In 08.1, focus on personal student exposure risks. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: an account, profile, device setting, website form, repository, domain record, or incident note. Connect it to teacher website security audit, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect personal student exposure risks back to website safety teacher sites. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: the action lowers a named risk, avoids unnecessary data collection, and gives the teacher a calm next step.

A reviewer should be able to find the personal student exposure risks decision, inspect the teacher website security audit evidence, and understand how it supports website safety teacher sites.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
