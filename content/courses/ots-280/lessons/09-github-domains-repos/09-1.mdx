# Secrets in Public Repos

## Why This Matters

Teacher safety work should reduce exposure when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **GitHub, Domains, and Public Repos**, the work sits inside teacher digital safety, account hygiene, public profile review, connection safety, and incident response. The risk is turning safety into fear instead of reducing specific exposure with practical routines. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Public repo and domain exposure check**.

## Core Idea

Treat this as a boundary lesson. Name what should be shared, what should stay private, what must be checked, and who is responsible for the final decision.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.
