# Repo and Domain Exposure Check

## Why This Matters

Teacher safety work should reduce exposure when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **GitHub, Domains, and Public Repos**, the work sits inside teacher digital safety, account hygiene, public profile review, connection safety, and incident response. The risk is turning safety into fear instead of reducing specific exposure with practical routines. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Public repo and domain exposure check**.

## Core Idea

Build the **Public repo and domain exposure check** as a usable artifact, not just a note. The finished version shows the decision, the classroom context, the evidence, and the next maintenance step.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is an artifact lesson, the teacher turns public repo domain exposure check into something another educator can use. The artifact needs labels, examples, status, review notes, and a next action instead of a vague promise to improve later.
