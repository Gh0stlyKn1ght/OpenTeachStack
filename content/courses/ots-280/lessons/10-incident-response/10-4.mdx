# Build Task: Incident Response Plan

## Core Idea

Incident Response for Teachers is about creating a first-hour plan for account compromise, phishing clicks, exposure, harassment, lost devices, or unsafe publishing. In this section, **Build Task: Incident Response Plan** turns that chapter focus into one practical teacher safety habit.

The goal is not to make teachers paranoid or technical for its own sake. The goal is to help a teacher decide who to contact, what to document, what not to touch, and when to escalate and leave behind a safe, maintainable artifact: **What to do if something happens plan**.

4. Record the teacher decision you made about who to contact, what to document, what not to touch, and when to escalate.
5. Save the result where it can support the chapter artifact: What to do if something happens plan.

## Safety Check

- Do not include student-identifiable information, passwords, recovery codes, MFA backup codes, account secrets, private links, hidden answer keys, or live incident details.
- Check for the chapter risk: deleting evidence, confronting attackers, delaying reports, or trying to handle serious danger alone.
- the artifact uses sanitized examples and does not expose private values.
- Escalate to district IT, administration, platform support, or appropriate authorities when the situation exceeds normal teacher maintenance.
- Keep a private version of sensitive notes and share only sanitized summaries for review.
