# Published Course Hub

## Why This Matters

A course site is a maintained teaching surface when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Published Course Hub**, the work sits inside course websites, static hosting, course hub structure, accessibility, privacy, and maintenance. The risk is treating a live website as finished when links, privacy, accessibility, source notes, and update routines are still unclear. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Published course hub**.

## Core Idea

Build the **Published course hub** as a usable artifact, not just a note. The finished version shows the decision, the classroom context, the evidence, and the next maintenance step.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is an artifact lesson, the teacher turns published course hub into something another educator can use. The artifact needs labels, examples, status, review notes, and a next action instead of a vague promise to improve later.

In 06.3, focus on published course hub. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a page, link, navigation item, resource entry, deployment target, or maintenance task. Connect it to published course hub, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect published course hub back to published course hub. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: students can find the current material, sensitive files are not public, links work, and the teacher knows when to update the site.

A reviewer should be able to find the published course hub decision, inspect the published course hub evidence, and understand how it supports published course hub.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
