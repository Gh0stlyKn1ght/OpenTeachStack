# Manual QA and Accessibility Checks

## Why This Matters

An agent can draft changes when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Testing and Verification**, the work sits inside safe coding-agent workflows for small teacher tools, prompts, diffs, tests, and review evidence. The risk is letting an agent own the instructional decision, overwrite authored content, or treat a passing build as the only proof. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Test checklist**.

## Core Idea

Treat this as a boundary lesson. Name what should be shared, what should stay private, what must be checked, and who is responsible for the final decision.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the manual accessibility checks decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

In 04.2, focus on manual accessibility checks. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a prompt, branch, diff, component, test result, tool screen, or review log. Connect it to test checklist, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect manual accessibility checks back to testing verification. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.
