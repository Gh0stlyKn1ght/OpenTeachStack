# Chapter Plan

## Why This Matters

An agent can draft changes when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Building Teacher Tools**, the work sits inside safe coding-agent workflows for small teacher tools, prompts, diffs, tests, and review evidence. The risk is letting an agent own the instructional decision, overwrite authored content, or treat a passing build as the only proof. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Small classroom tool**.

## Core Idea

Map the chapter before building. Choose one classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a prompt, branch, diff, component, test result, tool screen, or review log. Then name the classroom purpose it serves and the evidence that will show whether the work is ready.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is the opening plan for building teacher tools, the teacher names the sequence before creating files or artifacts. The plan separates what will be learned, what will be built, and what evidence will prove that the build is ready.

In 05.0, focus on chapter planning decisions. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a prompt, branch, diff, component, test result, tool screen, or review log. Connect it to small classroom tool, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect chapter planning decisions back to building teacher tools. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Common Mistake

A common mistake in **Chapter Plan** is treating the visible file or activity as proof that the teaching system is ready. The reviewer still needs to see the classroom purpose, the artifact evidence, the privacy or safety boundary, and the next revision trigger.

For this lesson, do not mark **Small classroom tool** ready until another educator can explain what changed, why it matters for **Building Teacher Tools**, and what would cause the work to be revised.
