# Chapter Plan

## Why This Matters

A capstone proves the system when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Capstone Scope**, the work sits inside capstone assembly across curriculum, automation, publishing, safety, peer review, and final release. The risk is calling the capstone finished because files exist even though reviewers cannot trace the decisions. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Capstone proposal**.

## Core Idea

Map the chapter before building. Choose one classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a capstone artifact, system link, evidence entry, safety review item, presentation slide, or release checklist row. Then name the classroom purpose it serves and the evidence that will show whether the work is ready.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is the opening plan for capstone scope, the teacher names the sequence before creating files or artifacts. The plan separates what will be learned, what will be built, and what evidence will prove that the build is ready.

In 01.0, focus on chapter planning decisions. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a capstone artifact, system link, evidence entry, safety review item, presentation slide, or release checklist row. Connect it to capstone proposal, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect chapter planning decisions back to capstone scope. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: a reviewer can trace the teaching need, inspect each artifact, see the safety checks, and understand how the system will be maintained.

A reviewer should be able to find the chapter planning decisions decision, inspect the capstone proposal evidence, and understand how it supports capstone scope.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
