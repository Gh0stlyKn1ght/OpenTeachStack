# Pathway Evidence Requirements

## Why This Matters

A capstone proves the system when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Capstone Scope**, the work sits inside capstone assembly across curriculum, automation, publishing, safety, peer review, and final release. The risk is calling the capstone finished because files exist even though reviewers cannot trace the decisions. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Capstone proposal**.

## Core Idea

Turn the lesson idea into an inspectable decision. Name the classroom use, the evidence, the review step, and the next revision trigger.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the pathway evidence requirements decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

In 01.2, focus on pathway evidence requirements. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a capstone artifact, system link, evidence entry, safety review item, presentation slide, or release checklist row. Connect it to capstone proposal, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect pathway evidence requirements back to capstone scope. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: a reviewer can trace the teaching need, inspect each artifact, see the safety checks, and understand how the system will be maintained.

A reviewer should be able to find the pathway evidence requirements decision, inspect the capstone proposal evidence, and understand how it supports capstone scope.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
