# Artifact Links and Reviewer Notes

## Why This Matters

A capstone proves the system when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Curriculum System Assembly**, the work sits inside capstone assembly across curriculum, automation, publishing, safety, peer review, and final release. The risk is calling the capstone finished because files exist even though reviewers cannot trace the decisions. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **System map**.

## Core Idea

Make the decision readable to someone else. The artifact explains the purpose, the source of truth, the review routine, and how future revisions will be recorded.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the artifact links reviewer notes decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

In 02.2, focus on artifact links reviewer notes. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a capstone artifact, system link, evidence entry, safety review item, presentation slide, or release checklist row. Connect it to system map, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect artifact links reviewer notes back to curriculum system assembly. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Artifact-Specific Reviewer Check

Before marking the system map row ready, the reviewer should be able to answer yes to all four checks:

- The artifact link opens for the intended reviewer.
- The artifact has a clear public/private boundary.
- The evidence proves classroom use or classroom readiness.
- The next revision trigger names an owner, date, or condition.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: a reviewer can trace the teaching need, inspect each artifact, see the safety checks, and understand how the system will be maintained.

A reviewer should be able to find the artifact links reviewer notes decision, inspect the system map evidence, and understand how it supports curriculum system assembly.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
