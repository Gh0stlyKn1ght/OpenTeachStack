# Final Release Checklist

## Why This Matters

A capstone proves the system when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Published Curriculum System**, the work sits inside capstone assembly across curriculum, automation, publishing, safety, peer review, and final release. The risk is calling the capstone finished because files exist even though reviewers cannot trace the decisions. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Published curriculum system**.

## Core Idea

Turn the lesson idea into an inspectable decision. Name the classroom use, the evidence, the review step, and the next revision trigger.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the final release checklist decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

Connect final release checklist back to published curriculum system. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Common Mistake

A common mistake in **Final Release Checklist** is treating the visible file or activity as proof that the teaching system is ready. The reviewer still needs to see the classroom purpose, the artifact evidence, the privacy or safety boundary, and the next revision trigger.

For this lesson, do not mark **Published curriculum system** ready until another educator can explain what changed, why it matters for **Published Curriculum System**, and what would cause the work to be revised.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: a reviewer can trace the teaching need, inspect each artifact, see the safety checks, and understand how the system will be maintained.

A reviewer should be able to find the final release checklist decision, inspect the published curriculum system evidence, and understand how it supports published curriculum system.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
