# Maintenance and Revision Plan

## Why This Matters

A capstone proves the system when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Published Curriculum System**, the work sits inside capstone assembly across curriculum, automation, publishing, safety, peer review, and final release. The risk is calling the capstone finished because files exist even though reviewers cannot trace the decisions. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Published curriculum system**.

## Core Idea

Make the decision readable to someone else. The artifact explains the purpose, the source of truth, the review routine, and how future revisions will be recorded.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is a focused section, the teacher isolates the maintenance revision decision from the rest of the course. The lesson asks what this idea changes, where it appears, and how the teacher will know it worked.

In 06.2, focus on maintenance revision. Choose one concrete classroom material or teacher decision from this list: a capstone artifact, system link, evidence entry, safety review item, presentation slide, or release checklist row. Connect it to published curriculum system, then decide whether it needs to be created, revised, tested, credited, restricted, published, or archived.

Connect maintenance revision back to published curriculum system. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: a reviewer can trace the teaching need, inspect each artifact, see the safety checks, and understand how the system will be maintained.

A reviewer should be able to find the maintenance revision decision, inspect the published curriculum system evidence, and understand how it supports published curriculum system.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
