# Published Curriculum System

## Why This Matters

A capstone proves the system when a teacher can point to the decision, the classroom use, the evidence, and the next review step.

In **Published Curriculum System**, the work sits inside capstone assembly across curriculum, automation, publishing, safety, peer review, and final release. The risk is calling the capstone finished because files exist even though reviewers cannot trace the decisions. This lesson narrows that risk into one visible update to **Published curriculum system**.

## Core Idea

Build the **Published curriculum system** as a usable artifact, not just a note. The finished version shows the decision, the classroom context, the evidence, and the next maintenance step.

The goal is not to make a perfect document in one pass. The goal is to make the teacher decision clear enough that another educator can inspect it, reuse it, or challenge it without needing a private explanation.

## Lesson Focus

Because this is an artifact lesson, the teacher turns published curriculum system into something another educator can use. The artifact needs labels, examples, status, review notes, and a next action instead of a vague promise to improve later.

Connect published curriculum system back to published curriculum system. That connection matters because a polished artifact can still fail if the lesson purpose, boundary, evidence, and maintenance routine are not visible.

## Final Release Checklist

| Release item | Evidence | Status | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Publish Decision

| Decision | When to use it | Example note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Publish | All public artifacts are privacy-safe, credited, accessible, and reviewable. | Publish the sample packet and keep real student evidence private. |
| Publish internally only | Classroom system is useful but includes school-only links or local process notes. | Share with department team; do not post public links. |
| Revise before publishing | One non-critical evidence, license, accessibility, or maintenance item is incomplete. | Add final license note before public release. |
| Block publication | Public packet includes private student data, restricted files, unsafe instructions, or unverified claims. | Remove restricted material and rerun safety review. |

## Common Mistake

A common mistake is treating "published" as a technical state instead of a review decision. A site can be online and still be unsafe, incomplete, uncredited, or impossible for another teacher to maintain.

Do not mark **Published curriculum system** ready until the release checklist shows what is public, what stays private, who owns maintenance, and what evidence would trigger revision.

## Quality Check

Before moving on, test the work against this standard: a reviewer can trace the teaching need, inspect each artifact, see the safety checks, and understand how the system will be maintained.

A reviewer should be able to find the published curriculum system decision, inspect the published curriculum system evidence, and understand how it supports published curriculum system.

If the work only names a tool or a file, revise it. The artifact needs to show the teaching purpose, evidence, boundary, and maintenance routine.
