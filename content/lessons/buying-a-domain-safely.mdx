# Buying a Domain Safely

Buying a domain should take 10 minutes and cost $10–15 per year. If it takes longer or costs more, something has gone wrong.

This lesson walks through the process step by step, focusing on what to do — and what to avoid.

## Step 1: Choose a Registrar

A registrar is the company where you purchase and manage your domain. Recommended options:

| Registrar | Price Range | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Cloudflare Registrar | At-cost ($8–10/yr) | No markup, excellent DNS, requires free account |
| Porkbun | $8–12/yr | Transparent pricing, clean interface |
| Namecheap | $8–13/yr | Beginner-friendly, good support |
| Google Domains (Squarespace) | $12–14/yr | Familiar interface for Google users |

**Avoid GoDaddy** for new purchases. Their checkout flow is designed to upsell unnecessary services, and their renewal prices are often significantly higher than the initial purchase price.

## Step 2: Search for Your Domain

1. Go to your chosen registrar's website
2. Type your desired domain name in the search bar
3. Check if it is available
4. If taken, try variations:
   - Different TLD: `.dev` instead of `.com`
   - Add a word: `mrcurriculum.com` → `buildcurriculum.com`
   - Use your name: `nevarez.dev`

<RealityCheck>
If your ideal `.com` is taken, do not overpay for it on the aftermarket. A `.dev` or `.org` works just as well for a teacher portfolio site. The content matters more than the TLD.
</RealityCheck>

## Step 3: Complete the Purchase

During checkout, you will be offered add-ons. Here is what to accept and what to decline:

| Add-on | Recommendation | Why |
|--------|---------------|-----|
| WHOIS Privacy | Accept (usually free) | Hides your personal info from public lookup |
| SSL Certificate | Decline | Your hosting provider handles this for free |
| Email hosting | Decline | Use Google Workspace or a free email forwarder |
| Website builder | Decline | You are building your own site |
| Domain protection/lock | Accept if free | Prevents unauthorized transfers |
| Auto-renewal | Enable | Prevents accidental domain loss |
| Multi-year registration | Optional | Saves a few dollars but locks you in |

**The total should be $8–15 for the first year.** If it is more, you have accepted unnecessary add-ons.

## Step 4: Post-Purchase Checklist

After buying your domain:

1. **Verify your email.** Registrars send a verification email. Click the link within 15 days or your domain may be suspended.
2. **Enable WHOIS privacy** if it was not included automatically.
3. **Enable auto-renewal** so you do not accidentally lose your domain.
4. **Do NOT change nameservers yet** — wait until you have hosting set up (covered in the DNS lesson).
5. **Save your registrar login credentials** in a password manager.

<TeacherNote>
The most common mistake new domain buyers make is purchasing unnecessary add-ons during checkout. Registrars make most of their profit on add-ons, not on domain registration itself. Teach students to read checkout pages carefully and decline anything they do not understand.
</TeacherNote>
