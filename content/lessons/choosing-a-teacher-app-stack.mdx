# Choosing a Teacher App Stack

A teacher app stack is the deliberate set of tools you use to build, manage, and deliver your curriculum. Not everything you have ever signed up for. Not the apps your district mandates. The tools you choose, maintain, and depend on.

## The Subscription Trap

Most teachers accumulate tools the way most people accumulate browser tabs — gradually, without intention, until the overhead becomes invisible.

A typical unexamined teacher tool list:

- Google Workspace (free via school)
- Canva (free tier)
- Kahoot (free tier, limited)
- Quizlet (free tier)
- Nearpod (school license)
- Pear Deck (trial expired)
- Notion (personal)
- Trello (forgotten)
- Padlet (free tier, limited)
- FlipGrid (absorbed into Teams)
- Remind (basic)

That is 11 tools. How many are you actively using? How many are you paying for? How many could disappear tomorrow without affecting your teaching?

<RealityCheck>
If you are paying for more than 3 education tool subscriptions, you are likely overspending on tools you underuse. Audit your subscriptions before adding anything new.
</RealityCheck>

## The Evaluation Framework

For every tool in your stack, answer four questions:

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Do I own my data? | Can you export your content in a standard format? |
| What is the total cost? | Free tier today, but what about next year? |
| Does it integrate with my existing tools? | Or is it a standalone island? |
| What happens if it disappears? | Can you migrate, or do you start over? |

## A Recommended Starter Stack

For most teachers, this stack covers 90% of needs:

**Core (Free, school-provided):**
- Google Workspace — Docs, Sheets, Slides, Forms, Drive, Classroom
- Google Calendar — Pacing and scheduling

**Communication:**
- Email (Google or school-provided)
- One messaging tool (Remind, Google Chat, or school-mandated platform)

**Content Creation:**
- Canva (free tier) — Visual materials
- One video tool — Loom, OBS, or screen recording built into your OS

**Automation:**
- Google Apps Script — Built into Sheets, free, powerful

**Publishing:**
- Your own domain + static site (Vercel/GitHub Pages, free hosting)
- Or Google Sites (free, limited but functional)

**Version Control (optional but recommended):**
- GitHub — Free for all teachers

That is 6-8 tools. Clean. Intentional. Mostly free.

<TeacherNote>
The stack above is a recommendation, not a mandate. Your context matters. If your school requires Microsoft 365, your core layer shifts. If you teach media production, you need video editing tools. The principle is the same: choose deliberately, audit regularly, own what you can.
</TeacherNote>

## Tools to Avoid (Usually)

Tools that typically create more problems than they solve for individual teachers:

- **Project management tools** (Trello, Asana, Monday) — Overhead exceeds value for a single-teacher workflow
- **Multiple note-taking apps** — Pick one. Google Docs or Notion, not both.
- **Gamification platforms** — Fun in demos, rarely sustainable across a semester
- **AI-everything tools** — Wait until you understand what you need before subscribing

<BuildTask>
Create a personal app stack audit:

1. List every education tool you use (free or paid)
2. For each, note: cost per year, whether you can export data, last time you actually used it
3. Identify tools to keep, tools to drop, and tools to replace
4. Design your ideal stack (8 tools or fewer)

Estimated time: 30 minutes
</BuildTask>
