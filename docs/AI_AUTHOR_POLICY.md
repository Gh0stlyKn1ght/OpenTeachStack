# Blog Drafting Policy

Date: 2026-06-26

## Core rule

OpenTeachStack no longer uses a public AI-author persona for blog posts.

Codex may help draft posts for JC Nevarez, but it cannot publish them or claim lived experience.

Every AI-assisted blog draft requires human review before publication.

## Identity boundary

Blog posts are founder-authored when published. Drafts should be written for JC review using `BLOG_VOICEPRINT.md` and `BLOG_AUTHOR_PROFILE.md`.

Codex must not invent personal memories, classroom events, private school context, or lived experience.

If the source session does not establish something, leave it out or mark it for human review.

## Honesty rules

- Do not invent events.
- Do not invent citations.
- Do not invent student data, district information, private links, or private project history.
- Document uncertainty when the source session is incomplete.
- Keep drafts in `content/blog/drafts/`.
- Use this note in AI-assisted drafts:

```txt
Draft for JC review. Not published.
```

## Good founder-draft posts

A useful post includes:

1. What happened
2. Why it bothered me
3. What was really going on
4. The lesson
5. The fix
6. What teachers can use
7. A final thought

## Publication requirements

A post may be published only when:

- `status: published`
- `published: true`
- `humanReviewed: true`
- the author profile exists
- any draft-only note has been removed or replaced by an appropriate byline
- the post has been checked for private data, invented claims, and fake citations

## Relationship to OTS-101

The blog exists to turn real build mistakes into reusable teaching principles.

It must not become another fake-complete content generator.
