# OTS-260 Media Accessibility Checklist

Date: 2026-06-22

Status: active checklist

Use this checklist for OTS-260 media packets, slide decks, lesson pages, diagrams, and short videos.

## Alt Text and Image Alternatives

- [ ] Every required image has alt text or nearby text that explains its instructional purpose.
- [ ] Decorative images are marked or treated as decorative.
- [ ] Diagrams include a text explanation of the same idea.
- [ ] Screenshots avoid private information and include enough context to be useful.
- [ ] AI-generated visuals are reviewed for misleading details and labeled in source notes.

## Captions, Transcripts, and Audio

- [ ] Required video has captions or a transcript.
- [ ] Important audio information also appears in text.
- [ ] Recording audio is clear enough to understand without strain.
- [ ] Video instructions are also available as written steps.
- [ ] Students have a fallback path if video is blocked or unavailable.

## Slide and Projector Readability

- [ ] Text is readable from the back of the room.
- [ ] Text is readable on a phone-sized screen.
- [ ] Contrast is strong enough for classroom lighting.
- [ ] Slides avoid long paragraphs and tiny screenshots.
- [ ] Color is not the only way information is communicated.

## Source, License, and Privacy

- [ ] Every borrowed image, video, icon, sound, or reading has a source note.
- [ ] License or terms allow the intended classroom or public use.
- [ ] Student names, faces, grades, messages, and private files are not visible.
- [ ] Generated or edited media is documented honestly.
- [ ] District media, consent, and publishing policies are followed.
