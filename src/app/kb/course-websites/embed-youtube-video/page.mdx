# Embed YouTube video safely

## The real problem

Video embeds can break layouts, lack captions, distract students, or point to the wrong audience.

## Quick answer

Use focused embeds, captions, transcripts when possible, and a fallback link.

## When to use this

Use this when adding a video to a lesson site, course chapter, or student direction page.

## Workflow

1. Watch the video first.
2. Check captions and age-appropriate content.
3. Embed only where it supports the task.
4. Add a fallback link.
5. Add a short teacher note about why the video is there.

## What to verify

Check captions, permissions, surrounding recommendations, and whether the embed works on mobile.

## Common mistakes

- Embedding before watching the full video.
- Using video as decoration.
- Forgetting a fallback link.
