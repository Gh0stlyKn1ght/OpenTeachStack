# VPN, Wi-Fi, and location privacy

## The real problem

Public networks, location sharing, and personal devices can reveal more context than teachers expect.

## Quick answer

Use trusted networks, keep devices updated, avoid sensitive work on unknown Wi-Fi, and review location sharing.

## When to use this

Use this when working from conferences, cafes, hotels, or shared public spaces.

## Workflow

1. Prefer a trusted hotspot or school-approved VPN. If policy allows paid tools, this program is available as an option: [NordVPN](https://refer-nordvpn.com/niMjnaKCzMR) (3 months for each qualifying signup).
2. Avoid logging into sensitive systems on unknown Wi-Fi.
3. Confirm sites use HTTPS.
4. Turn off unnecessary location sharing.
5. Keep your device and browser updated.

## What to verify

Check local policy before using VPNs or personal hotspots with school systems.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming all password-protected Wi-Fi is safe.
- Sharing screenshots with location or account details.
- Ignoring browser update prompts.
