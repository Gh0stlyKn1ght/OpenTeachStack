# Turn standards into learning targets

## The real problem

Standards are often pasted into plans without becoming teachable, observable targets.

## Quick answer

Pull out the verb, content, context, and evidence. Then write a target students can understand.

## When to use this

Use this before building lessons, assessments, rubrics, or AI prompts from standards.

## Workflow

1. Copy the exact standard into your planning notes.
2. Highlight the action verb.
3. Name the knowledge or skill.
4. Decide what evidence would prove learning.
5. Write the student-facing target.
6. Check that the activity actually produces that evidence.

## What to verify

Do not let the template overwrite the teacher. The target must still fit your class and your assessment.

## Common mistakes

- Keeping the standard wording as the student target.
- Writing an activity before evidence is clear.
- Claiming alignment without checking the verb.
